Why we careI thought we could do this thing

The bet
Treat a pile of related prediction markets as one joint probability distribution — and sample from it.
Trading for grounding
Real positions keep the model honest. If the math is wrong, the market collects the fee. No grading your own homework.
Rich data
Markets price hundreds of overlapping, oddly-shaped quantities — marginals, seat counts, conjunctions, linear payouts — updated continuously.
Capabilities ⇄ requirements
Trading is the requirement; the model is the capability. Each round of trades demanded better math; each round of math made new trades expressible.

Politics isn't the most interesting application. It's the easiest one to start on — dense markets, clean resolution, a single date when everything settles.

The requirement/capability loop is the real design philosophy: the arb bot forced exact AMM math; exact math made trades safe; safe trades justified a richer model; the richer model demanded better sampling; better samples enabled trades nobody could express before. Neither half survives alone.

The core reframe“This market is mispriced” is a type error

✗  “This market is wrong.”  — wrong about what? It's the price.

✓  “This set of markets is inconsistent.”  — that type-checks.

  • A valid trade names two-plus markets and a property they disagree about — and bets on the inconsistency resolving.
  • You never need external truth. Only markets disagreeing with each other.
  • Done right, you profit whether D or R wins.
For an EMH-aware room this is provocative without triggering the reflex — we're not claiming to out-predict a market on what it directly prices. We're claiming the SET is incoherent and nobody is jointly arbitraging it. Discussion hook to plant for later: where's the line between aggregating the market and overruling it?

The object of attentionA market is a marginal. You want the joint distribution.

Every market is a one-dimensional shadow of some joint distribution over world-states. We read them one at a time — but the questions worth asking live in the joint distribution:

  • What's P(Dems take the Senate | they lose Georgia)? No market prices it.
  • Which race most moves P(control) per donor dollar? A correlation query.
  • Lock in a Georgia Democratic win — what does that pull the other lean states to? Conditioning, live.
  • How correlated are the tails? Wave years are a co-movement story no marginal shows.

A few joints are traded — the 5-way chamber-control market prices the Senate×House 2×2 directly. Everything finer isn't. So: rebuild the joint distribution the markets are shadows of, then query it.

Reframes their daily object of attention. Once someone sees "the joint gives me X for free," they start listing their own X's — the state we want for the discussion half. NOTE: the live viewer does HARD conditioning (lock an outcome, rejection-filter the sample cloud). SOFT belief injection — "GA is really 60/40" as a min-KL tilt — is NOT built yet (on the punch-list; see last-minute-list.md). Demo the lock, pitch the soft tilt as a wishlist item in discussion.

SystemThe loop

Markets and model share one language — payout functions on S. Anything you can write as a φ, the model can price; anything the model prices, you can trade back. Post-trade refits find no remaining edge: the loop closes.

The state-space box is the slide's real content: a market IS an indicator function with a price pinned to it. Once markets and model speak the same language, the loop follows — model anything you can trade, trade anything you can model. Say out loud: (1) everything between snapshot and samples is model-agnostic — the constraint registry is the interface, elections are just one model; (2) the gold dashed arrow is what most "forecast" projects don't have. Trade 011: found M$54 of guaranteed edge, traded it, re-solved — edge gone (−M$2.34). The market was actually moved to consistency. Ground truth no backtest gives you.

Mechanism — one slide, more belowMaximum entropy, then sample hard

  • Each market price is an expectation constraint on the state space: E[φᵢ(s)] = priceᵢ — works for any market shape.
  • Fit the maximum-entropy joint distribution matching every price: P(s) ∝ exp(Σ λᵢ φᵢ(s)) — the least-committal distribution consistent with what's traded. Where markets are silent, it adds nothing.
  • Constraints overlap and conflict → learned λ via iterative fitting with Tikhonov regularization; the residuals that won't go away are the inconsistencies.
  • Sampling: Metropolis-Hastings + parallel tempering (16-rung ladder, T=∞ at the top) — because election night is multimodal: blue wave / red wave / split are separated basins.
  • Full Congress refit: 2×10⁹ MH steps, ~2 h on 16 cores. The sampler theory ate three months of our lives.
  • Press ↓ for the full bag of tricks. Or just ask.
Depth-on-demand: literally ask the room — "want the machinery, or shall we keep poking the outputs?" If they bite, the vertical slide below has the full inventory. Honesty if pressed on convergence: R-hat on seat counts still ~1.2–1.5 pooled; the binding uncertainty is rare-cluster excursions, and replication-implied true ESS is more like tens than thousands. Depth on demandThe full bag of tricks

  • Parallel tempering, 16 rungs — ladder frozen at the Polyak log-mean of an adaptive run's settled interior
  • T=∞ top rung — free mode-hopping; per-cycle correlation ρ ≡ 0 by construction — plus a T=20 hot anchor
  • DEO non-reversible swap schedule (Syed et al.), swaps every 5 steps
  • Per-rung adaptive proposal rates targeting 0.234 acceptance; proposal flattening toward uniform at hot rungs
  • Unbroken replica trajectories across refit iterations (continue_chains) — slow-mode statistics become measurable
  • Warm starts everywhere — λ from Polyak time-averages, kernel state and per-rung inits from previous runs' pools
  • Jacobi-preconditioned λ updates, LR schedule 0.1 → 0.05, Tikhonov regularization
  • Pool over the last 10 of 20 iterations — valid by a rearrangement theorem: pooled-over-drifting-λ ⇔ frozen-at-the-mean
  • Honest diagnostics: per-functional ESS, R̂, excursion-anatomy Neff = N/(1+CV²), ladder-resolution Λ̂

Every one of these exists because a run failed without it.

If anyone asks "why not just use Stan/NUTS": discrete 470-dim state space, no gradients, multimodal — this is the regime where tempering is the tool. The deeper finding: textbook PT prescriptions (0.234 swap targets, equi-AR ladders) silently assume a lumpability property our landscape breaks. That's its own paper-shaped object.

The honest slide“More confident than the market” — two different claims

(a) Price ≠ probability
Even one market's price is a biased estimator: compression toward 50%, favorite-longshot bias, cost of locked capital until resolution. We read through the bias when we fit. This edge is real and free.
(b) Inconsistencies persist
Arbitrage is gated — for big players by liquidity, leverage, Kelly asymmetry. For us, it's cadence and depth, not bankroll: each trade exhausts the local edge, and refilling it needs an hours-long refit. Spotting ≠ harvesting.

Estimate-accuracy and tradable-edge are different claims. Keep them separate and you can be honest about both.

We are explicitly NOT bankroll-limited: effective Kelly bankroll ~M$100k dwarfs trade sizes (hundreds of M$); γ/Kelly is "nearly free." The real binders: (1) LIQUIDITY/DEPTH — the concave sizer self-saturates where "markets cannot absorb" more, often only M$177–1,400 net per trade; (2) CADENCE — a trade consumes the available inconsistency, so harvesting more requires a fresh ~2h refit, capping us at ~1–2 trades/day (bounded by sampler wall-clock). Model-confidence and latent-liquidity caps are deliberate governors pending the external-validation program, not a capital shortage. Source: tasks/curvature_aware_sizing/findings_*. Discussion hook: when should you trust a synthesized joint over a thick, liquid single market — and when is that hubris? The humility earns the room.

A cautionary tale from last weekConverged ≠ correct

  • A thin Louisiana seat-count market priced in redistricting risk our per-seat priors knew nothing about.
  • The fit reconciled the only way maxent knows how: smear the risk symmetrically across LA seats — corr(LA-02, LA-06) ≈ 0.1. Nobody's belief. Internally consistent. Externally wrong.
  • Self-play can't create information. A model that trades only against markets it already ingested will converge — to coherent nonsense — if a mechanism is missing.
  • The fix needs no conditional market: ingest the mechanism market — P(new LA map) = 0.72 (Callais) — pin the branch everyone already knows (P(LA-06 = D | current map) ≈ 0.99)…
  • …and the contested conditional falls out by arithmetic from two cheap unconditional prices.

The rule it taught us: conditional markets are only needed when both branches are uncertain. One near-deterministic branch makes plain markets fully informative. (Worked out on paper — not yet wired into the live model.)

Three lessons packed in one story: (1) joints reveal WHERE risk lives, not just how much; (2) internal consistency is not truth — converged ≠ correct; (3) identification theory becomes a market-authoring rule. STATUS HONESTY: the diagnosis (symmetric smear, corr≈0.1) is real and computed; the fix (ingest mechanism market + pin known branch → derive the conditional) is worked out but NOT yet implemented in the model or viewer — it's the recipe, not a deployed feature. Discussion hook: which conditionals in your domain are identified for free, because one branch is already known?

Generative direction #1Build the markets you wish existed

  • The pain: full coverage of a joint outcome space is unaffordable — most cells draw no natural interest and need subsidies nobody pays.
  • The reframe: maxent tolerates partial coverage. Market design stops being “cover every outcome” and becomes choose a sparse constraint basis.
  • The design question goes information-theoretic: which market buys the most constraint per subsidy dollar? Built: a v0 ranking (price-deviation × mutual information) that picks which market to author next. True value-of-information — entropy reduction ÷ subsidy cost — is designed, not yet built.
  • The further reach (concept): flip taker → maker — price bespoke conjunctions off the joint, hedge in the liquid components, charge for the residual. A structured-products desk for prediction markets.
Honesty on status: the v0 ranking script (deviation × MI, in tasks/extensions_brainstorm_20260610/voi_v0_ranking.py) runs and ranks markets — but it's explicitly NOT full VOI (no prior over the would-be price, no subsidy-cost denominator). "Be the house" / OTC is pure concept — the ingredients exist (joint gives conjunction prices + hedge ratios; arb bot has redemption machinery) but nothing assembles them. Q&A magnet. Hooks: "five markets to pin a ten-dimensional forecast — which five?" and "what bespoke bet would you want priced?" Bolton Bailey builds the market-side half; we have the model-side half — the symbiosis is real.
